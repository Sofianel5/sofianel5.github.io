Thesis
Although tragedies like the triangle shirtwaist factory fire of 1911 showed there was still much work to be done
to improve the lives of labourers in the early 1900s,the uprising of the 20,000 broke barriers by for the first time having a strike
largely lead by female labourers and organizers. Before the strike, women were largerly seen as being unable to organize,
or as detrimental to the labour momvement. But by organizing the largest general strike the country had ever seen,
women proved their worth to unions, and were recognized finally as peers.
