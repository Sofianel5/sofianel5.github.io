This belief spread all the way up to the Supreme Court, in its ruling of Lochner vs. New York. Lochner was an employer who claimed that the NY Legislature‚Äôs Bakeshop Act of 1985, which helped to regulate the conditions and working time in bakeries, prohibiting them from working more than 10 hours a day, was unconstitutional.
